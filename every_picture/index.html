Well...at least part of it.

Visual Snow Syndrome (VSS) is a rare neurological condition that impacts the brain and can cause the person impacted to have a distorted visual field. This can include seeing tv like static across the visual field, after images, floaters, trails of light, and more.

Aside from visual symptoms, individuals with this condition tend to have tinnitus, symptoms of disassociateion, trouble with processing, anxiety, light sensitivity, and more.
